Breath Support
All articulation flows from your breath. Diaphragmatic breathing gives your words power, clarity, and control without tension in the throat.
👄
Lip & Tongue Precision
Crisp consonants require active, deliberate lip and tongue placement. Lazy articulators create mumbled, indistinct speech.

Resonance & Placement

Step 1 of 4 — Warmup
Breath & Body Warmup
Take 5 slow belly breaths. On each exhale, release on a gentle "hahhh." Feel your jaw, neck, and shoulders soften. Your voice needs a relaxed body to work from.
"Haaah... Mmmm... Vvvvv..."
Lip trill · Hum · Fricative buzz
Now do 20 seconds of lip trills — blow air through relaxed, vibrating lips. This warms your articulators without strain.
Step 2 of 4 — Precision Drill
Consonant Crispness
Speak each phrase slowly 3×, then at normal pace 3×, then quickly 3×. Exaggerate lip and tongue movement — feel each consonant snap.
"Red lorry, yellow lorry, red lorry, yellow lorry."
Focus: L · R distinction — tip of tongue
TIME YOUR ROUND
0:30
Step 3 of 4 — Vowel Extension
Resonance & Clarity
Sustain each vowel for 5 seconds on one breath, placing your hand on your cheeks. You should feel vibration. Open your mouth wide — vowels live in open space.
"AY — EE — AH — OH — OO"
Forward placement · Open jaw · Feel the buzz
Then speak this phrase as slowly and clearly as you can, savoring every vowel: "Around the rugged rock the ragged rascal ran."
Step 4 of 4 — Applied Speech
Read Aloud Challenge
Read the passage below aloud with full intention. Speak at 75% of your normal speed. Exaggerate clarity without sounding robotic. Record yourself if possible — playback is powerful feedback.
"Whether the weather be fine or whether the weather be not — whether the weather be cold or whether the weather be hot — we'll weather the weather, whatever the weather, whether we like it or not."
Rate · Rhythm · Clarity
Reflect: Which sounds felt unclear? Make a mental note to focus on them tomorrow.
